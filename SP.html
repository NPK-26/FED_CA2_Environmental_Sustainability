School Campus

Singapore Polytechnic was established in 1954 and is the first and oldest polytechnic in Singapore.
Over the years, the campus has gone through many changes to be what it is today.

  • SP has 4 certified Green Mark Platinum Buildings with efforts to have more certified.
  • The campus also has 230 species of Flora. Including the infamous Brown Woolly Fig Heritage Tree located near Food Court 1. Students can admire the diverse scenery as they walk to their classes.
  • SP has smart sensors to monitor the facilities to make sure the school environment is comfortable for both students and staffs, and their health and well-being.
  • In order to encourage eco-friendliness, SP has ceased distributing plastic water bottles at events and installed water dispensers for students and staff alike to use instead.
  • The Polytechnic also has plans to install photo-voltaic panels across campus to harness renewable clean energy.

SP Environment Club

"The SP Environment Club (SPENV) is a CCA group concerned about the natural environment. SPENV is committed to promoting environmental awareness and a sustainable lifestyle both on campus and beyond. We organise many green campaigns and projects meant to make a difference in SP, such as roadshows and out of school trails." - SP official website.
You can find out more about them on Instagram at @sp_env or email them at sp_env@ichat.sp.edu.sg

Common Core Curriculum (CCC) for Students

The CCC consists ten modules designed to make students into critical thinkers, have an understanding of the sustainability issues and the problems faced by both local and global communities, and be challenged to make change with the skill taught in CCC in order to build a better and more sustainable world.
They are:

  • Thinking Critically about the UN SDGs
  • Data Fluency
  • Artificial Intelligence and Its Impact
  • Persuasive Communication with Storytelling
  • Problem Solving with Creative and Computational Thinking
  • Digital Communication for Impact
  • Collaboration in the Digital Age
  • Effective Writing for the Workplace
  • Personal Branding and Career Agility
  • Sustainable Innovation Project

SP's Recycling Efforts

SP also has created many ways to recycle the old to make the new. The two most prominent examples are:

  • Using Plastic Waste to build roads

    "Local construction company Samwoh has teamed up with SP researchers and students to use plastic waste in asphalt to build more durable road surfaces. Research scientist Lim Zheng Bang and research engineer Estee Tan of SP's Advanced Materials Technology Centre, and Dr Handojo Djati Utomo and Dr Chan Chin Loong, lecturers at the School of Architecture and the Built Environment, worked with two groups of students to test the asphalt made with the plastic pellets. The team found that asphalt made from plastic waste could last at least 20 per cent longer than regular asphalt, and had levels of chemical leaching that were within official guidelines. This is the first time such pellets have been used in creating asphalt in Singapore. The team will continue its research, and hopes to test its product on a stretch of road soon." - SP (20 Jan 2020)

  • Launch of first Solar Photovoltaics recycling line

    "The first solar photovoltaics recycling line is being piloted at SP. The system involves a machine to take apart solar panels, so its components - including precious materials like silver and glass - can be recycled. It has a target recycling capacity of 80 panels a day and a recovery rate of up to 95%. Dr Thong Ta Xuan, Senior Research Scientist from TIE was interviewed. The Polytechnic has also established two laboratories - SP-Sembcorp Solar PV Research Lab and SP-CHINT Smart Electrical Power Training Laboratory to enhance research and development in the field of solar energy." - SP (01 Nov 2022)
