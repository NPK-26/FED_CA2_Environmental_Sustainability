Conserve Water

DIFFICULTY: ☆☆☆☆

Water is a natural resource that we use everyday but it is also a limited natural resource. Take shorter showers instead of taking a bath. When doing your laundry, run a full load in the washing machine. The same goes for using the dishwasher and don't rinse your plates before you run it

Stay Informed and Share the News

DIFFICULTY: ☆

Stay ahead by reading the latest news, whether by social media or news sites. Make sure they are from credible sources or else you will be decieved by misinformation! Don't just read the news. Share them with everyone. Talk about the issues that are happening globally. Let your friends and family be aware of climate change, the fights for equality and poverty. Let them know that they should speak up to get the changes needed to reach our goal.

Choose what you buy carefully

DIFFICULTY: ☆☆☆

Making a difference in what you buy can help. If you buy goods with minimal packaging, you are reducing plastics that end up in landfills. Buy more energy efficient light bulbs to reduce use of electricity. Many fruits and vegetables go to waste because people won't buy them for their unconventional shapes. Persuade your parents or family to buy those funnny shaped fruits. While you are at it, support the local businesses by shopping local. It keeps people employed and save trucks from going on long-distance drives.

Power-Saving

DIFFICULTY: ☆☆

When there is a light not in use, turn it off. Plug all of the appliances into a power strip to easily turn it off when you are not using any of them. Use more energy efficient models of appliances and use energy-saving light bulbs instead.

DIFFICULTY: ☆☆☆☆

Convince your household to install solar panels if you have the option to do so. As a bonus, it saves the electric bill.

Reduce Plastic

DIFFICULTY: ☆☆☆☆☆

UN reports:
"Approximately 7 billion of the 9.2 billion tonnes of plastic produced from 1950-2017 became plastic waste, ending up in landfills or dumped."
Plastic is a big problem and it's everywhere. Although it's difficult to completely avoid it, we can do our best to minimise using it in our lives. Start by taking a water bottle everywhere with you instead of buying a new bottle made of plastic. Bring your own bag when shopping. Recycle when you can. You can do this!
